CO2 Emissions and Global Climate Change

Carbon dioxide emissions are the primary driver of global climate change.

It's widely recognised that to avoid the worst impacts of
climate change, the world needs to urgently reduce emissions.

In this visualisation we will look at CO2 Emmisions per Capita
by different Countries over the time and
it's affect on the global temperature.


On the Map above the darker the colour the higher the CO2 Emission per Capita.
Hover on the map for more details or change the year by slider

On the graph on the left we can see the change in Average World's CO2 Emissions.
It started from 3.12 in 1960 and went to 4.48 in 2018.

On the graph on right we can see the CO2 Emissions of all the countries , the life expectency and the Population.
We can notice that countries with higher Population tend to have more CO2 Emissions.

Change the population and continent to sort of the values.


In the above two graphs we can see the top 10 Countries with
Highest CO2 Emmisions and Highest Increase in CO2 Emissions.

From 1960 to 1985 Aruba was at the top
and then Qatar maintained the rank 1.

We can see that with the increase in CO2 Emissions
the Average Temperature is also increasing.


This is indication of Global Warming and if it's not controlled
it will result in more frequent hot days and fewer cool days.
The Natural calamities will become more common and
more severe leading to major health crisis.
